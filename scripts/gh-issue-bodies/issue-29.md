## Objective

Preserve the long-range evaluation ladder for IMPACT so future benchmark work remains coherent, staged, and governed instead of turning into disconnected score theatre.

## Unified Context

**Benchmark and evaluation are a future programme layer.** **Current MLP activation and dashboard truth come first.** This issue preserves **long-range programme structure**, not current benchmark execution authority.

IMPACT is currently focused on the MLP path:
- public install truth
- local app usability
- hosted ingest
- real aggregate dashboard data
- honest release/distribution activation

That means benchmark work is **not** the current execution bottleneck.

This roadmap card exists to preserve the future programme shape from:
- discovery and environment inventory
- atomic reliability and exactness
- capability validation
- benchmark discipline and eventual compare-vs-crowd surfaces

without allowing benchmark ambition to outrun product truth.

## Theme / Goal

Keep the future evaluation ladder visible and structured.

The long-range goal is to move from:
1. discovery
2. atomic reliability
3. capability checks
4. benchmark programme maturity

in a deliberate order.

## Why this matters

Without a visible evaluation ladder:
- benchmark work can become ad hoc
- teams may jump to scoring before the foundations are trustworthy
- future comparison features may drift away from the actual product architecture
- the programme may overclaim maturity before the system has earned it

## What this does not mean yet

This issue does **not** mean:
- benchmark execution starts now
- benchmark overlays belong on the public web now
- scores matter more than installation, ingest, and dashboard truth
- compare-vs-crowd is a current execution priority

This is a future-shaping roadmap card, not an active delivery ticket.

- create pseudo-scientific benchmark theatre instead of a rigorous programme
